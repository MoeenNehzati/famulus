# Research Presentation: Mathematics Deviations

- Relative to the general presentation baseline:
  - the main question, result, and proof architecture should guide the talk early
  - after the introductory portion, specialist talks can focus on the new results, but broader talks need more explicit motivation and narrative structure
  - the talk should usually prioritize one coherent theorem-story over trying to reproduce the paper's full logical coverage
  - definitions and intermediate claims should be introduced only when their role in the story is already visible
- Common additional failure mode:
  - the audience cannot tell the role of intermediate results
  - the talk starts with formal setup before the audience knows what phenomenon or theorem is worth following
