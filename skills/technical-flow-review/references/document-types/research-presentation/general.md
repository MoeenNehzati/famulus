# Research Presentation: General

- Primary goal:
  - help listeners follow the core story in real time
- Shared flow expectations:
  - the beginning should orient the audience immediately
  - the narrative should be simpler and more selective than a paper
  - each part should help the audience retain the central thread
  - the talk should complement the paper by giving a higher-level and more informal overview rather than reproducing the paper's full detail
- Shared burden:
  - very high and very early motivation
  - very high signposting
  - filtered technical detail
- Shared constraints:
  - the presentation should fit the allotted time
  - some of the allotted time should be reserved for questions, transitions, or technical glitches
  - listener processing limits are binding
  - optional or backup material should be easy to drop
- Common failure modes:
  - trying to read like a paper
  - too much technical detail before orientation
  - weak narrative thread
