# Journal Article: Mathematics Deviations

- Relative to the general journal baseline:
  - the immediate goal, larger goal, and role of the current section should be especially visible
  - precise theorem architecture can appear earlier without harming readability if orientation is preserved
  - each major milestone should ideally be formalized prominently enough to support high-level understanding
  - section openings should often state the purpose of the section and the role of the upcoming milestone
  - the exposition should make clear not only what is true, but why each major construction or lemma is entering the argument
- Common additional failure mode:
  - the reader cannot tell why a lemma, construction, or section is being introduced
  - the paper is logically correct in local chunks but globally hard to navigate
