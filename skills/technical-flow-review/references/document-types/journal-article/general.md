# Journal Article: General

- Primary goal:
  - present a durable research contribution
- Shared flow expectations:
  - the title, abstract, and introduction should make the problem, contribution, stakes, and takeaways visible early
  - the paper should be organized for the reader rather than the author's discovery order
  - major milestones should be visible before too much technical detail accumulates
  - the opening should make clear the motivation, question, improvement over prior work, and the evidence or results the reader should remember
- Shared burden:
  - high motivation
  - high signposting
  - high rigor/detail
- Shared constraints:
  - length should be disciplined
  - routine or peripheral material should not crowd the main thread
  - technical components should be pushed later when possible to ease the learning curve
- Common failure modes:
  - contribution visible too late
  - technical development before orientation
  - structure that buries the main thread
