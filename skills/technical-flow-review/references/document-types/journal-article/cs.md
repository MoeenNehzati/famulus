# Journal Article: Computer Science Deviations

- Relative to the general journal baseline:
  - the problem framing, method-level contribution, and intended takeaway should become clear especially quickly
  - the contribution should be positioned efficiently
  - multiple audiences are common, so the paper should be explicit about its take-home messages and background burden
  - the paper should say clearly what the technical advance is relative to prior work and what kind of evidence or argument supports the claim
  - long technical sections should still carry clear reminders of the main question and the claimed payoff
- Common additional failure mode:
  - the setup is too diffuse, or the contribution is never stated sharply enough
  - the reader sees many components but never gets a clean account of the central advance
