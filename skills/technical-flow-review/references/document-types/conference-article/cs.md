# Conference Article: Computer Science Deviations

- Relative to the general conference baseline:
  - the problem, method, and takeaway should become clear almost immediately
  - the abstract and introduction should state the central technical idea sharply enough that a fast reader can tell what is new
  - the paper should tell the reader early what the claimed advance is relative to prior work and what evidence supports it
  - because conference readers often read under time pressure, the paper should give strong forward pointers and repeated orientation in the technical sections
- Common additional failure mode:
  - no sharp central claim
  - the contribution is scattered across technical details instead of distilled near the front
