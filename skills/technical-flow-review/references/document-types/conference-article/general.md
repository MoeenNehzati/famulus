# Conference Article: General

- Primary goal:
  - communicate a contribution under tighter space and attention constraints
- Shared flow expectations:
  - the reader should know quickly what the contribution is and why it matters
  - the paper should identify its main claim, main evidence, and main takeaway earlier than a journal article typically can
  - the main body should prioritize the central thread over exhaustive detail
  - compression should sharpen the storyline rather than densify every section
  - the introduction and abstract should work especially hard because many readers will decide quickly whether the paper is worth close attention
- Shared burden:
  - high but compressed motivation
  - very high signposting
  - selective rigor/detail in the main body
- Shared constraints:
  - space is tight
  - compression should not destroy the main narrative
  - only the material that is needed to understand and trust the central contribution should stay in the main thread
- Common failure modes:
  - overcompression
  - too much machinery for the available space
  - no crisp high-level takeaway
  - key evidence or key theorem statement arrives too late for the reader to orient
