# Conference Article: Mathematics Deviations

- Relative to the general conference baseline:
  - the main problem and result should become visible quickly enough to preserve proof architecture under compression
  - the paper should still tell the reader what the main idea is, not just present a shortened chain of lemmas
  - compression should preserve the logical map: what the main theorem is, what the key new ingredient is, and what is being deferred
  - when details must be omitted, the exposition should still make the proof strategy and the role of intermediate results legible
- Common additional failure mode:
  - technical compression destroys the mathematical architecture
  - the reader sees a dense technical excerpt rather than a coherent theorem-driven story
