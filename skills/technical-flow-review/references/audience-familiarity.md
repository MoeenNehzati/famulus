# Audience and Familiarity

Use this file for default expectations coming from audience and reader familiarity.

These defaults interact with document type and field/subfield.

Before using a familiarity label, ask:
- What does this reader already know?
- What does this reader need explained?
- What is this reader supposed to remember, decide, or do after reading?
- Is there more than one meaningful audience, with one group setting the main standard and another requiring translation?

## Self

- Background burden:
  - minimal
- Motivation burden:
  - low
- Signposting burden:
  - low, but future readability still matters
- Common failure:
  - notes become unusable after time has passed

## Coauthor

- Background burden:
  - moderate to high shared context can be assumed
- Motivation burden:
  - moderate
- Signposting burden:
  - moderate
- Common failure:
  - assumptions about shared knowledge become too implicit, so the note becomes legible only to the author

## Advisor

- Background burden:
  - substantial, but not unlimited
- Motivation burden:
  - moderate
- Signposting burden:
  - moderate to high
- Common failure:
  - the point of the note or draft is not obvious quickly enough, especially what is established, blocked, or wanted from the reader

## Field specialist

- Background burden:
  - high
- Motivation burden:
  - still meaningful, but more compressed
- Signposting burden:
  - moderate to high
- Common failure:
  - important structure is left implicit because the author assumes too much familiarity

## Broader same-field researcher

- Background burden:
  - moderate
- Motivation burden:
  - high
- Signposting burden:
  - high
- Common failure:
  - the document assumes subfield-specific context too early

## Adjacent-field researcher

- Background burden:
  - lower should be assumed
- Motivation burden:
  - high
- Signposting burden:
  - high
- Common failure:
  - the document offers neither enough translation nor a clear reason to care
